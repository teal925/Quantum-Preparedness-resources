# Tip 7 - If you find something you love, chase it down! #
Finding your niche not only makes it easier to enjoy your work or studies, but also can set you apart in a positive way by bringing some special expertise and passion to the table.

# Tip 8 - See Something, Say Something #
Calling out positive and negative behaviours when you or others around you experience them can help demonstrate what good looks like, and clearly articulate what can be improved. If you have the right privilege, platform, seniority etc. at work to be able to speak up, advocating for yourself and others will help drive change.

# Inspiring the next generation #
During the panel, we mentioned programs that help girls and women to study and begin their careers in cyber, however did not touch on how the younger girls who are still at school might hear about cyber. In WA, we have a number of programs that do school outreach and present cyber as a possible career path to young people. These include:
* https://www.witwa.org.au/initiatives/techtrails/ Women in Tech WA (WiTWA) Techtrails Youth Program includes school incursions and presenting tech and cyber careers
* https://mawainc.org.au/events/ Mathematical Association of Western Australia (MAWA) hosts Winter School Programs and Maths Empowering Girls day, which highlight careers where maths is used - including cyber security and cryptography
